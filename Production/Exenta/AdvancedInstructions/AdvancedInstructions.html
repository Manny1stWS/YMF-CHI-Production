EXENTA ADVANCED SETUP/NAVIGATION

Prepared by Manuel Herrera

Updated: 2/17/24

SHORTCUTS AND NAVIGATION VIEWS:

You can setup shortcuts by clicking the Search Icon on the top left side of Exenta:

Then in the “Search Application” you can type what you want to set up as a shortcut.

For Example, you can type “Style Master” then click and drag Style Master from the left side to the right side. After you do this whenever you click the Search Icon on top left, you will have a Tile with Style Master that you can click on for easy access.

Clicking the Search Icon on the top left size of Exenta also gives you access to customize the way Exenta looks and/or displays your shortcuts.

Section A displays the last 5 tables you have accessed in Exenta.

Section B “My Favorites Launcher” allows you to always have access to all your shortcuts on the left side of the screen.

Section C “My Settings” allows you to change theme colors used in the application and you can change the “MenuOption” from “Traditional” which is the standard view which has the navigation on top:

Or “MenuOption” “Outlook” which will have the navigation on bottom left and all your favorites shortcuts on top left:

If you want to search for multiple Styles in the Style Master, you can easily copy them from an excel file and paste them in Exenta and Exenta will separate each style with a comma as long as each style is in a separate cell. For example:

Copy the styles you want to search from your excel file:

Paste them in the Style field and then click the “Apply Filter” icon :

The Styles you are searching for will display in the “Result” tab of the Style Master:

In order for you to show specific columns, you will need to right click on one of the columns then click columns and Show/Hide Columns:

Then you can move the “Hidden Columns” on the left to the “Displayed Columns” on the right by selecting what you want to move than then clicking > or < or moving the displayed columns up or down. Be careful not to click the >> or << because these buttons will move everything.

These are some of the definitions of the displayed columns in “Prod Order Detail” that might not be self-explanatory:

  1. Stage: We are currently using 3 Stages:
    1. Proform: this is the initial stage showing open balances.
    2. Boat: these are shown after containers packing lists have been uploaded.
    3. Rec: these are shown after containers have been received.

  3. Moved Qty: This shows the quantities that have been moved between stages.
  4. Moved Qty: This shows the quantities that have been moved between stages.

INVENTORY REPORT:

You can check inventory by going to Inquiries > Item Management Inquiry:

In here you can search by any of the fields displayed and then clicking search:

If you want to search multiple styles, you can list the styles you want to search for and separate them with a comma.

Then the system will display all the styles in all the different Whs Groups or different Variations of Label, PPK and/or Size Codes if there were multiple variations setup in one Style:

These are some of the definitions of the displayed columns that might not be self-explanatory:

  1. QOH: These are the quantities currently on hand before customers’ orders are shipped.
  2. Open Qty: These are the quantities customers have ordered for this style but have not yet been picked by the system.
  3. Alloc Qty: The quantities that have been allocated from the Open Qty and what’s available from QOH after Pick Qty. Note that the Alloc Qty is still part of the Open Qty.
  4. Pick Qty: These are the quantities that have been picked by the system.
  5. Avail QOH: These are the quantities available after Open Qty and Pick Qty.
  6. Open Intransit Qty: These are the quantities “WIP Qty” that have been shipped but not received and are in “BOAT” Stage in a Purchase Order in the Production Order Maintenance.
  7. Open PO Qty: These are quantities “WIP Qty” that have not been shipped and are in the “PROFORM” Stage in a Purchase Order in the Production Order Maintenance.
  8. WIP Qty: These are all the quantities “WIP Qty” in “BOAT” and “PROFORM” Stage that have not been received yet in a Purchase Order in the Production Order Maintenance.
