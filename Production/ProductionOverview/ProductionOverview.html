PRODUCTION GENERAL GUIDE

Prepared by Manuel Herrera

Updated: 1/12/24

OVERVIEW & PURPOSE

Give production coordinator a general understanding of the requirements to place and follow up on orders.

EXPECTED FROM PRODUCTION COORDINATOR

  1. Know how to use Microsoft Excel.
  2. Know how to do basic formulas in excel.
  3. Have basic math skills.
  4. Be able to make conversions using the metric system. For example converting inches to centimeter, find the square meter of a product, finding the grams per square meter of a product, and converting box dimensions to cubic meter.
  5. Be detail oriented.
  6. Be able to communicate and work well with others.
  7. Be very organized.
  8. Learn quickly.
  9. Take very good notes.
  10. Remember what was taught to you.
  11. Getting familiar with customers' guidelines and requirements to ship orders. Vendors guides are normally found in the customer's website. If you cannot find it, ask the orders' manager or salesman to get you a vendor's guide.
  12. Getting familiar with products and the factories that produce them.

OBJECTIVES

  1. Placing orders with vendors.
  2. Getting Proforma Invoice (PI) from vendors.
  3. Getting Proforma Invoice signed to send back to vendors.
  4. Emailing signed Proforma Invoice back to vendors.
  5. Getting the signed Proforma Invoice stamped by vendors.
  6. Getting and approving lab dips, preproduction samples (pp samples) and Top of Production samples (TOP samples) from vendors.
  7. Getting and approving inspection reports.
  8. Making sure orders are shipping on time.
  9. Getting and approving shipping documents (Packing Lists and Commercial Invoices).
  10. Making sure orders are arriving to destination on time (specially customer orders).
  11. Keeping your work in progress (WIP) updated.
  12. Keeping Exenta updated.
  13. Occasionally make reports of orders placed/shipped.
  14. Give updates to managers, salesmen and/or buyers about customer orders.

TOOLS NEEDED

  1. A YMF’s Microsoft Outlook email account (Email IT Department helpdesk@ymfinc.com if you need access).
  2. Access to Exenta (Email IT Department helpdesk@ymfinc.com if you need access).
  3. Location to the Excel files used to create Style#s and assign UPC#s. (See File Locations Page)
  4. Location to where Purchase Orders (PO) are saved. (See File Locations Page)
  5. You will need access to FineLine. This is where we order price stickers for customers that require price stickers. Ask the Production Manager to give you access to this website.
  6. You will need access to the “Packaging Form Log” file in Teams. Ask the Production Manager to give you access to this file.

ORDERS DISTRIBUTION AND INFORMATION

Orders are received in various ways:

  1. You might get an email from the customer with information about what items and quantities the customer wants to order written on the email.
  2. You might get an excel with items and quantities the customer wants.
  3. You might get a PDF Customer PO by email.
  4. You might be asked to access a customer’s website to download an order from the customer.

If you are not asked directly by Leadership to place an order, make sure to check with them to make sure it is OK to place the order.

Regardless of how you get a customer’s order, it is important you know if that order will be received via Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) directly into our system.

Customers like TJX, Costco, Burlington, Big Lots, Family Dollar, and Old Time Pottery normally send their orders via EDI directly to Exenta.

When orders are received via EDI it is crucial to provide customers with the corresponding Style#s for the items the customer wants to order. These Style#s must be entered into Exenta within 5 business days to ensure seamless processing and prevent system-related issues. Failure to input the Style#s before EDI reception may result in notifications from the EDI team.

Upon receiving an order from a customer, salesman, or manager, the critical step is to promptly place the order on the same day with the essential information. This allows for the generation of a proforma invoice from the vendor, providing pricing details and a commitment to deliver the goods by the specified date. Timely action ensures efficient processing and facilitates necessary arrangements with the vendor.

If you do not get the essential information needed, please communicate with Leadership about the missing information.

DETAILS OF OBJECTIVES

  1. Placing orders with vendors:

    • Purchase orders need the following:

      1. Essential information needed to place the order:

        1. YMF PO number (Generated from Exenta).
        2. Quality specification of the item. The more detailed, the better. Showing things like the % of materials in the item. Listing different components of the item and the weight of each component and or at least listing the total gram per sqm weight of the item.
        3. Sizes of the items being ordered. The actual sizes of the product are listed in centimeters for most products. We normally also list the sizes in inches (a roundup of the actual centimeter size).
        4. Style#s.
        5. Description, designs and colors.
        6. Quantity.
        7. Case Pack.
        8. Case Type (Box, Ppbag, PDQ, etc).
        9. Box quantity.
        10. Square meter (when applicable).
        11. Square meter price and Unit price.
        12. Total Amount.
        13. Requested Sailing Date. AKA Estimated Time of Departure (ETD).
        14. Destination Port.
        15. Requested Arrival Date.

      2. Not needed to place the order but must be provided at least 45 days before agreed ETD:

        1. Customer po# (if applicable).
        2. UPC (if applicable).
        3. Customer sku# (if applicable).
        4. Box barcode(if applicable).
        5. Warehouse Code.
        6. CADs, picture of product or spec sheet.
        7. Packaging, sewn labels, law labels, brand labels and their placements.
        8. Box labels and their placements.
        9. Note that Ecom items must be individually polybagged. The polybag must have a suffocation warning printed on it.

  2. Getting Proforma Invoices from vendors:

    1. You should try to get the proforma invoice in no more than 3 business days after emailing your PO to a vendor. If it takes you longer, you must inform the manager that gave you the order.
    2. The proforma invoice must have a list of the Style#s & Descriptions you are ordering, and including the materials and quality of the items, price per square or (price per piece only when price per square meter is not applicable), total amount, ETD, origin and destination ports, cubic meter of each item, total cubic meter, vendor’s name, and vendor’s bank information. We prefer that vendors follow our standard proforma invoice. (See File Locations Page).
    3. The PI must have payment terms of 100% against documents or 100%TT but there are times when vendors require deposit and depending on the vendor, we will accept no more than 10% deposit and 90% against documents. Pay close attention to payment terms in PI and point it out to the manager signing the PI if a deposit is required.

  3. Getting Proforma Invoice signed to send back to vendor:

    1. After checking the proforma invoice has the info listed previously and checking total amount matches, you must get the PI signed by Yaron or Jonathan the same day you get the PI. If Jonathan or Yaron do not give you time to sign the PI’s after 3 business days of attempting to get them to sign your PI, talk to your production manager about it.
    2. You can print the PI and have Yaron or Jonathan signed your PI, or you can submit the PI using this website: https://smallpdf.com. Ask the production manager for username and password.
    3. Vendors will not honor the proforma invoice if the PI is signed too long after receiving it.
    4. For Yaron or Jonathan to sign PIs, sometimes they will ask to show them a margin sheet. Talk to the production manager about how to create a margin sheet.
    5. The production manager can sign PI’s but only if Yaron or Jonathan had signed a previous PI with the same price and quality.
    6. Make sure you point out the payment terms if the vendor is requesting a deposit.

  4. Emailing signed Proforma Invoice back to vendor:

    1. You must scan and save the signed PI in the proforma invoice folder in the production file server. This will allow the import department to easily find it to process payment to vendors once goods are shipped. (See File Locations Page).
    2. Check the scanned PI, if the document is not clear, save the original PI that you received in the proforma invoice folder in the production server.
    3. If the PI requires deposit, you must email the signed PI to APaccounting@ymfinc.com, jpatel@ymfinc.com and ahalbe@ymfinc.com and ask them to process the payment of the deposit. A lot of times PI will say shipping date is 50 days after receiving deposit. Vendors will not honor the ETD in the PI unless they receive the deposit with enough time.
    4. If the vendor is new (Vendor is not in Exenta), you must ask the vendor to fill out “VendorSet-upForm”. (See File Locations Page). Once you receive the form from the vendor, email it to Jay Patel jpatel@ymfinc.com along with the signed PI and ask him to setup this new vendor.

  5. Getting the signed Proforma Invoice stamped by vendor:

    1. This will solidify the agreement between us and vendor.
    2. A lot of times the first time you get the PI, it will already have the vendor's company stamp.
    3. Also save this file in the proforma invoice folder in the production file server.

  6. Getting and approving lab dips and preproduction samples from vendors:

    1. The very first time you order an item from a vendor; you must request lab dips to make sure colors will meet our standards.
    2. You should try to get Lab dips in no more than 1 week after the order is placed.
    3. Once you receive the lab dips, review them with the design team.
    4. If the design team does not approve the first lab dips and you get a 2nd round of lab dips and they do not approve the 2nd round, you must get leadership involved since asking for lab dips a 3rd time might get the order delayed.
    5. Preproduction samples are extremely important especially if it is the first time when we are buying a product from a vendor.
    6. You should try to get pp samples in no more than 2 weeks after lab dips are approved. Notify the leadership involved with the order if it takes longer. This usually means the order will be delayed.
    7. Once you get the pp sample, the first thing you need to do is measure and weight the product to check the grams per square meter (GSM) to check if matches the quality ordered.
    8. Schedule a meeting with the designers involved in the development of the item and Jonathan or Yaron to get an approval.
    9. If the pp sample is not approved, always be aware about when the customer needs the goods and how asking for another pp sample would affect the order’s ETD. Get leadership involved if pp samples are not approved the 1st time you get them since asking for revised pp samples a 2nd time might delay your order.

  7. Getting and approving Inspection Reports:

    1. You should try to get inspection reports at least 14 days before the agreed ETD. If you do not get an inspection report 14 days before the ETD, it is usually a sign the order will be late. Try to get info from the vendor about the order and let leadership know if the order is late. We have in house inspectors for orders shipping from China, India, and Turkey. Orders you place from other countries will require you to contact a 3rd party inspection company to schedule the inspection well in advance.
    2. Review and comment on inspection reports the same day you get them.
    3. In the inspection, you should see pictures of the boxes, the box labels, where the box labels are placed, measurement of the boxes, headers on the product, closeup of the UPC or price stickers on the header, measurements of the product, weight of the product, defects found. You should check all these things against the order you place and approve the inspection if everything matches the order.
    4. If you see any issues, you must inform the leadership to determine if a chargeback is required.
    5. Email vendor about the chargeback and have them confirm it.
    6. Make sure the import department is included in emails approving inspection report or discussion about chargebacks.

  8. Making sure orders are shipping on time:

    1. Follow up with our teams in China, Turkey, and India to make sure containers are booked at least 30 days before the agreed ETD in PI.

  9. Getting and approving shipping documents (Packing Lists and Commercial Invoices):

    1. Make sure vendors send you, our import department, and Account Payable shipping documents within 2 business days of the goods being loaded in containers.
    2. You will need to check the packing list to make sure it has the necessary information such as container#, loading date, sailing date, ETA, correct destination ports, correct warehouse code, correct po#, correct quantity, correct customer po#.
    3. You will need to check the commercial invoice matches the items and quantity of the packing list and the price matches the signed PI.
    4. Email the import department confirming that you checked the shipping documents, and everything looks good. Make sure this is done at least 2 weeks before ETA of containers.
    5. If we need to issue a chargeback because of issues, let the import department know of the chargeback.

  10. Making sure orders are arriving to destination on time (specially customer orders):

    1. The ETA on the shipping documents is normally close to the actual date the container will arrive at the port.
    2. For POE orders, it is recommended the ETA is 2 or 3 business days before the customer’s ship date.
    3. If the ETA on the packing list is within the customer ship window, the order should be considered on time.
    4. You should check the container status on the carrier's website 2 or 3 days after the sailing date to check the ETA has not changed. Take a screenshot of the ETA on the website in case we need to show proof the container was on time. Make sure the date is on the screenshot in case the ETA changes later.
    5. If the ETA changes and it is on or after the cancel date, you need to notify the leadership.
    6. You should track your containers at least once a week until the containers arrive.
    7. Check with the import department to make sure POE orders are routed on time. Normally customers ask for POE orders to be routed at least 7 days before ETA.

  11. Keeping your work in progress (WIP) updated:

    1. You must enter information about your POs in the WIP right after PI is signed.
    2. Having a WIP will help you organize and be able to follow and know the status of various aspects of your orders.

  12. Keeping Exenta updated:

    1. You must make all the required data entry every week into Exenta. It is recommended that you do the data entry after PIs are signed.
    2. Make sure the Style#s created are entered in the Style Master in Exenta. You need to fill out and email the “New Style Form for Data Entry Team” to njodataentry@ymfinc.com for help with this.
    3. If you get a customer order via email, you must email njodataentry@ymfinc.com the style#s, YMF PO#, and file location where the YMF PO# associated with the customer PO was saved so they can help with these entries.
    4. The data entry team will help enter the YMF PO# also if all the necessary information is emailed to them.
    5. Make sure the YMF POs have the correct warehouse code, expected receipt date, style#s, quantities, and prices from signed PI’s.
    6. Weekly check your POs in Exenta to make sure there is no remaining balance in POs that you know will not ship in the future. You need to do this after the packing lists of your order has been imported into Exenta. Check with the import department to make sure the packing lists were imported.

OTHER IMPORTANT INFO:

  1. Work with orders’ manager and/or salesman on what packaging to use for orders.
  2. Fill out a form for the packaging team and email it to them. You should also enter PO information in the Packaging form log in teams. You should do this the same day you send the order to a vendor.
  3. Unbranded packaging normally takes 10 business days from submit date and branded packaging normally takes 15 business days.
  4. When you get packaging artwork back from the packaging team, please make sure all the information you provided to them for the packaging is correct in the artwork. Information like Size, Material, Care Instructions, etc…
  5. Make sure you ask Leadership what type of samples the customer requires (pp samples, top samples or both) and how soon the customer needs them.
